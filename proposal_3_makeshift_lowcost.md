# Proposal 3: Makeshift, Low-Cost Warehouse
**For:** Upcoming Fashion Brand — Warehouse Design  
**Model:** Lean, Fast, Bootstrapped — Minimum Viable Warehouse  
**Prepared:** 2026-05-16  

---

## Executive Summary

This proposal sets up a functioning warehouse using the absolute minimum viable investment — primarily standard retail clothing racks, basic shelving, a low-cost cloud inventory tool, and smartphone-based barcode scanning. It is designed to be operational within **5–7 days** of taking possession of the empty space, at a total setup cost of **$2,500–$6,000 (₹2–₹5 lakhs)**.

It is not elegant and it will not scale beyond 1.5× current volume without significant rework. However, it works — and it keeps cash in the business for product, brand building, and marketing during the critical early months when revenue is still uncertain.

This proposal is explicitly a **bridge solution**. Every part of this design includes an "upgrade path" marker that shows exactly which component to replace first when budget improves. Follow this path honestly, and transition to Proposal 2 within 12–18 months.

**Best for:** Brands in pre-revenue or very early revenue stage; bootstrapped founders without confirmed investment; brands where the warehouse is a new addition and the team has no prior warehouse operations experience and needs to learn the basics first.

---

## A Note of Honesty

Industry research (Prediko, Uphance, Shopify, NetSuite) is clear: small fashion brands that avoid inventory systems entirely — managing stock in Excel or WhatsApp — routinely face stockouts, wrong dispatches, double-selling, and returns fraud. This proposal avoids those pitfalls while minimising upfront spend. **A cloud inventory app is non-negotiable even in this proposal.** Everything else can be basic. Inventory software cannot.

### 1.3 Layout Principles (Kept Simple)

- **Flow left-to-right or U-shape.** Receiving at one end; dispatch at other end. Storage in middle.
- **Aisle markings mandatory** — use yellow duct tape or floor paint for all walking paths. Minimum 3 ft secondary aisles, 4 ft primary. This is the cheapest safety investment and non-negotiable.
- **Zone signs** — print A4 signs laminated: "RECEIVING", "JEANS", "DISPATCH", etc. Stick on wall with tape. Costs under ₹500.

## 2. Storage Systems

### 2.1 Hanging Storage — Freestanding Garment Racks

**System:** Heavy-duty commercial freestanding garment racks (double-hanging bar preferred)

**Not** the flimsy chrome racks from retail stores — use heavy gauge steel freestanding racks rated for 150–200 kg capacity.

**Specification:**
- Quantity: 20–25 racks
- Type: H-frame double-bar hanging rack (2 rails, one above the other) — doubles capacity per rack footprint
- Dimensions per rack: 1.2 m wide × 1.8 m tall × 0.5 m deep (double bar)
- Capacity per rack (double bar): ~60–80 garments
- Total hanging capacity: 20 racks × 70 garments = **~1,400 pieces**
- At 25 racks: ~1,750 pieces

**Organisation:**
- Each rack gets a card with: Category | Style Code | Size Range
- Organise within rack by size (left to right: XS → XL → XXL)
- FIFO: clip a clothespin at the "first pick" end of each rack (all new stock added at back, picks taken from clothespin end)

**Cost:**
- Heavy-duty double-bar garment rack: $25–$50 each
- 20 racks: $500–$1,000 / ₹41,500–₹83,000

**Upgrade path → Proposal 2:** When you have budget, swap these racks for industrial GOH fixed-frame rail system. GOH holds more per sq ft, is more secure, and integrates with barcode/RFID systems. The freestanding racks can be repurposed in the store or sold.

### 2.2 Folded Storage — Wire Shelving or Basic Longspan

**Option A (cheapest):** NSF-certified heavy-duty wire shelving units
- $80–$120 per unit (4-shelf, 1.8 m tall × 1.2 m wide × 0.46 m deep)
- 10 units: $800–$1,200
- Capacity: 10 × 4 shelves × 25 pieces = ~1,000 pieces
- Good for ventilation (no moisture build-up on garments)

**Option B (slightly better):** Entry-level steel longspan shelving
- $200–$300 per bay (2.1 m × 1.8 m × 0.6 m, 5 shelves)
- 8 bays: $1,600–$2,400
- Capacity: 8 × 5 × 30 pieces = ~1,200 pieces
- More sturdy; accommodates deeper stock stacks

**Recommendation for this proposal:** Start with wire shelving for jeans and knitwear (affordable, ventilated), then replace with longspan at first opportunity.

**Jeans Zone Setup (4 shelf units):**
- Dedicate 4 wire shelf units exclusively to jeans
- Each shelf labelled with waist × leg size (e.g., "W30 L30", "W32 L32")
- Visual: write size on masking tape; stick to shelf edge — cheap and functional
- Keep a tally card per size showing current stock count (update manually after each pick or daily count)

**Upgrade path → Proposal 2:** Replace wire shelving with longspan + add bin labels with barcodes.

### 2.3 Packing Area

- 2× heavy-duty folding tables (or a secondhand workbench)
- 1× label printer on small side table
- Packing supplies stored on a spare wire shelf unit adjacent to the table
- 1× basic postage scale (kitchen-style postal scale works at this volume)

## 3. Technology Stack

### 3.1 Inventory Management (Non-Negotiable Even in Makeshift)

**Recommended: Zoho Inventory (free to $59/month)**

Why Zoho Inventory:
- Free plan for up to 50 orders/month; $59/month starter for higher volumes
- Mobile app with barcode scanning using phone camera (eliminates hardware scanner cost initially)
- Handles multi-channel (Shopify integration on paid plan)
- Tracks stock by SKU, location, and variant (size/colour)
- Purchase orders, transfer orders (warehouse → store), returns — all built in
- Cloud-based — accessible from any device; no server needed

**Alternative: Shopify Inventory (if already on Shopify)**
- If the brand uses Shopify for online orders: Shopify itself has built-in inventory tracking at no additional cost
- Supplement with a Google Sheet "Warehouse Stock Log" for physical counts
- This is the cheapest possible path, but loses transfer-order functionality

**Alternative: inFlow Inventory ($89–$149/month)**
- Good barcode workflow
- Better for physical warehouse tracking than Shopify's basic inventory

**Do not use Excel as the primary inventory system** — acceptable as a supplementary tool (e.g., for weekly count crosscheck) but fatal as the sole source of truth at 5,000 pieces with high SKU count.

### 3.2 Barcode Scanning — Smartphone First

- Install Zoho Inventory (or inFlow) mobile app on staff smartphones
- The app's camera functions as a barcode scanner — works with EAN-13 and Code 128 barcodes
- When scanning volume becomes painful (>50 scans/session): buy 1 Bluetooth barcode scanner (Honeywell 1900 or inateck BCST-70: $30–$80) paired with phone

**When to upgrade:** When daily pick volume exceeds 30–40 online orders, smartphone scanning becomes the bottleneck. That's when you buy dedicated corded barcode scanners ($80–$150 each).

### 3.3 Label Printing

**Minimum viable setup:**
- 1× thermal label printer: Zebra GK420d or Brother QL-820NWB
  - Zebra GK420d: ~$200–$280 (professional, ribbon-based, long-lasting labels)
  - Brother QL-820NWB: ~$150–$200 (cheaper, direct thermal, great for shipping labels)
- Labels: 4×6 inch thermal shipping labels ($15–$25 per 500-roll)
- For hang tags and bin labels: print on paper, laminate with a $15 laminator, attach with S-hooks or tape

**What to print:**
- Shipping labels (for online orders — auto-generated by Zoho/Shopify + courier integration)
- Location labels for shelves/racks (print once; laminate)
- SKU bin cards for shelves

**Cost: $150–$280 for printer + $30–$50/month consumables**

### 3.4 Courier Integration

- Use **Shiprocket** (India) or equivalent aggregator platform
- Shiprocket integrates with Shopify, WooCommerce, and others
- Auto-fetches order details; prints shipping label; sends tracking to customer
- Manages multiple couriers (BlueDart, Delhivery, Ekart, Ecom Express) from one platform
- Pricing: pay-per-shipment, no subscription needed at low volume
- This eliminates manual label typing — critical even in a makeshift setup

### 3.5 No WMS — Compensate with Rigid Manual Discipline

At this tier, there is no WMS. This means:
- **Location discipline is everything.** Every item must be in its labelled location. No ad-hoc storage.
- **Daily brief** by warehouse manager: what came in, what went out, any discrepancies.
- **Weekly count** of fast-moving SKUs is mandatory to catch shrinkage early.
- **Pick list = printed or WhatsApp message** from manager to associate. Format: Order ID | SKU | Size | Location | Qty.

## 4. Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

Even with minimal technology, strict written SOPs are essential. Studies show that warehouses without SOPs see 15–20% higher error rates (GoAudits, 2024). These SOPs are designed to be printed as laminated A4 sheets and mounted at each zone.

---

### SOP 1: RECEIVING (Mount this at the Receiving Zone)

**Step 1 — Before goods arrive:**
- Get dispatch note from manufacturing/procurement (WhatsApp message is acceptable; must list: style name, SKU, size, quantity).
- Confirm expected arrival time.

**Step 2 — On arrival:**
- Unload all goods into receiving zone.
- Do not move to storage until count is complete.

**Step 3 — Count:**
- Count every piece. Tally against dispatch note.
- Any discrepancy: photograph, note on a discrepancy sheet, call manufacturing within 2 hours.

**Step 4 — Visual QC:**
- Check 20% of pieces (random sample) for: brand hangtag, stitching quality, correct size label.
- Defective pieces: put in red bin labelled "QUARANTINE". Do not stock.

**Step 5 — Log in inventory system:**
- Open Zoho Inventory app.
- Create "Receive Items" entry: select supplier, enter SKU, quantity.
- Scan barcodes or enter manually.
- Save. Inventory count updates.

**Step 6 — Putaway:**
- Move garments to correct rack/shelf location.
- Hanging: add to back of correct rack section.
- Folded: add to back of correct shelf (newest at back, oldest at front).
- Update tally card if applicable.

**RULE: Receiving is not complete until all items are in the inventory system AND in their labelled physical location.**

---

### SOP 2: PUTAWAY LOCATION RULES

Each location in the warehouse has a label. The label contains:
- Zone letter (H = Hanging, F = Folded/Shelf)
- Category code (JN = Jeans, TS = T-Shirt, DR = Dress, etc.)
- Rack/bay number
- Example: `H-DR-07` = Hanging zone, Dresses, Rack 7

Every SKU has one home location (listed in inventory system and on the SKU's bin card).
- If home location is full: place in overflow location; write it on the bin card and update inventory system.
- Never place items on the floor or on random surfaces.

---

### SOP 3: ONLINE ORDER PICKING

1. Order notification arrives (email/Shopify notification/Shiprocket dashboard).
2. Manager or associate opens order. Writes/types pick list: Item | SKU | Size | Location.
3. Pick from listed location. Take from the **front** of the rack (oldest stock first, FIFO).
4. Bring item to packing table.
5. Visually confirm: item matches order (style, size, colour).
6. If mismatch: do not pack. Return item; re-check inventory system location.

---

### SOP 4: STORE REPLENISHMENT PICKING

1. Store manager sends replenishment list (WhatsApp or shared Google Sheet).
2. Warehouse associate picks items and places in a carton.
3. Write a transfer note (paper):
   - Date | From: Warehouse | To: [Store Name] | Items: [list with quantities]
4. Both associate and manager sign transfer note (2 copies; 1 stays at warehouse).
5. Update Zoho Inventory: create Transfer Order, mark as "completed."
6. Carton sealed and labelled.

---

### SOP 5: PACKING — ONLINE ORDERS

1. Garment at packing table.
2. Final check: compare garment size tag with order details.
3. Fold neatly; wrap in tissue if branded packaging required.
4. Place in mailer or box.
5. Print shipping label from Shiprocket (or courier app).
6. Apply label. Place on dispatch staging floor zone (marked by floor tape — 1 zone per carrier).

---

### SOP 6: DISPATCH

1. Carrier arrives at agreed pickup time.
2. Count all parcels staged for this carrier.
3. Carrier agent signs a handwritten or printed manifest (copy remains with warehouse).
4. Mark orders as "dispatched" in Zoho Inventory / Shopify.
5. Shiprocket auto-sends tracking to customer if integrated.

---

### SOP 7: RETURNS PROCESSING

1. Return arrives. Record: Order ID, item, reason for return (from customer note or return form).
2. Inspect garment:
   - **Grade A** (unused, tagged): refold, scan back into inventory ("Return to Stock" in Zoho).
   - **Grade B** (tried, minor): put in Returns Assessment bin. Manager reviews weekly — markdown or outlet.
   - **Grade C** (damaged): log in Returns Write-Off sheet. Manager authorises disposal/donation.
3. Initiate refund/exchange in Shopify or e-commerce platform.

---

### SOP 8: DAILY STOCK TALLY (Manual Kanban)

For jeans (evergreen high-SKU zone):
- Each size position on shelf has a tally card
- After each day's dispatch, associate updates tally card: subtract outbound, add any received
- If count on tally card drops to "**REORDER LEVEL**" (set by manager, e.g., 10 per size): peel a RED sticky note and stick it visibly on the shelf. This tells manager to raise a replenishment order.

For other SKUs:
- Weekly count (Friday end-of-day): scan or count all SKUs; compare to inventory system.
- Discrepancy >5% for any SKU: investigate immediately (mis-pick, mis-stock, theft).

---

### SOP 9: HOUSEKEEPING

**Daily:**
- Clear packing table at end of shift.
- Return all empty hangers to designated hanger bar (one small section reserved for empty hangers on each GOH rack or on a dedicated spare rack).
- Sweep packing and dispatch zones.
- Put all carton waste in trash.

**Weekly:**
- Check all rack stability (wobble test on freestanding racks — tighten any loose bolts).
- Check fire extinguisher pins.
- Confirm exit path clear.

**Monthly:**
- Deep clean entire floor.
- Inspect wire shelving for any bent wires or sharp edges.
- Review laminated zone signs — replace any that are worn or peeling.

## 5. Staffing & Roles

### 5.1 Minimum Viable Team (2–3 Staff)

At this scale and technology level, a smaller team is both feasible and necessary (budget constraint).

| Role | Count | Monthly Cost (INR) | Key Responsibilities |
|------|-------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Warehouse Manager / Supervisor** | 1 | ₹25,000–₹40,000 | Operations lead; inventory system admin; picking + packing oversight; reporting; store coordination |
| **Warehouse Associate** | 1–2 | ₹12,000–₹18,000 | Receiving; putaway; picking; packing; housekeeping |

**Total monthly payroll: ₹37,000–₹76,000 / ~$450–$915**

**Note:** At 2,900 pieces/month inbound and ~30–50 online orders/day, a 2-person team is sustainable with strict SOPs and a lean, disciplined operation. A 3rd person is added when daily online orders exceed 60 consistently.

---

### 5.2 Warehouse Manager/Supervisor — Full JD

**Reports to:** COO / Founder  
**This role is the single point of accountability for the warehouse.**

**At this tier, the manager also does associate-level work** — they will pick, pack, and receive alongside the associate(s). This is normal and expected in a lean/startup operation.

**Responsibilities:**

*Daily operations:*
- Receive incoming goods; update inventory system; execute putaway
- Process online orders: generate pick lists; oversee packing; coordinate Shiprocket dispatch
- Process store replenishment: generate transfer notes; coordinate carton delivery
- Manage returns: grade, restock, or write off

*Inventory:*
- Weekly manual count of fast-moving SKUs
- Maintain inventory system accuracy (Zoho Inventory or equivalent)
- Flag slow-moving stock to brand team (bi-weekly report)
- Maintain jeans tally cards; raise reorder flags

*Coordination:*
- Daily WhatsApp update to COO/founder: units received, orders dispatched, any issues
- Coordinate with store managers for replenishment requests
- Liaise with Shiprocket/courier for pickup SLA issues

*People:*
- Schedule associate work; assign daily tasks
- Train associate on SOPs (use laminated SOP cards mounted at each station)

*Reporting (Weekly, to Founder/COO):*
- Units in / units out
- Online orders dispatched
- Current inventory level (snapshot from Zoho)
- Any stockout or overstock flags
- Returns volume

**Skills Required:**
- 1–2 years any warehouse, retail, or logistics experience
- Basic smartphone literacy (Zoho app, WhatsApp, Shiprocket)
- Organised and methodical — in a makeshift setup, personal discipline is the system
- Willing to do physical work alongside administrative work

**Salary Note:** This role may initially be filled by a founder/operations team member part-time as the warehouse builds volume, then replaced by a full-time hire when daily operations become too heavy for a shared responsibility.

---

### 5.3 Warehouse Associate — Full JD

**Reports to:** Warehouse Manager  
**Physical role:** On feet for most of shift; lifting up to 10 kg.

**Responsibilities:**
- Assist receiving: count, carry garments to receiving zone, help with QC
- Putaway: carry garments to correct rack/shelf; update tally cards
- Pick online orders: follow pick list; bring to packing table
- Pack online orders: fold, wrap, apply label
- Pack store replenishment cartons
- Housekeeping: daily and weekly tasks
- Alert manager when any rack/shelf label is missing or damaged

**Skills:** Physically active; careful; follows written instructions (SOP cards are provided); basic literacy.

---

### 5.4 Part-Time / Seasonal Flex Staff

During peak (festive season, sale events), add 1–2 casual/contractual workers for 2–4 weeks:
- Source through local employment agency, Apna app, or WorkIndia platform
- Give them a single SOP card (packing only, or receiving only — don't train across all zones)
- Manager supervises directly
- Cost: ₹600–₹900/day per casual worker

## 6. Compliance & Safety Standards

Even at the lowest cost tier, these non-negotiables apply:

| Requirement | Why | How to Implement (Low Cost) |
|-------------|-----|----------------------------|
| Aisle markings | Prevent accidents; OSHA best practice | Yellow duct tape on floor (~$5/roll, 3 rolls) |
| Fire extinguisher | Legal requirement; fire risk in textile storage | 3× 2 kg CO2 extinguishers ($30–$50 each); annual service |
| Emergency exit marked | Legal requirement | Print "EXIT" sign + arrow; laminate; affix above door ($2) |
| Smoke detector | Fire early warning | 3× standalone battery-powered smoke detectors ($10–$15 each) |
| Racking load placard | Even for basic racks, know their rated load | Ask supplier for rating; write on masking tape label on rack |
| Labour registration | Shops & Establishments Act | Register with local municipal authority; PF/ESI for 5+ employees |
| GST compliance | E-way bill for stock transfers >₹50,000 | Generate on GST portal; file with transfer note |
| Footwear | Closed-toe shoes mandatory in warehouse | Communicate to all staff; provide 1 pair safety shoes to associate if needed |

## 8. The Critical Upgrade Roadmap

This is a bridge, not a destination. Follow this upgrade sequence:

### Phase 1 (Month 1–3): Get Operational
- Set up racks, shelves, zones, SOP cards
- Onboard Zoho Inventory; enter all SKUs
- Start scanning all inbound and outbound
- Establish daily inventory update habit

### Phase 2 (Month 4–8): Fix the Friction Points
**At the first signs of pain:**
- Picking errors increasing → Add dedicated corded barcode scanners ($80–$150 each)
- Inventory count taking too long → Consider Linnworks or Uphance (Proposal 2 WMS)
- Racks becoming unstable or crowded → Replace with GOH fixed rail (Proposal 2 storage)
- Manager overwhelmed → Hire a third associate

### Phase 3 (Month 9–18): Transition to Proposal 2
- Replace garment racks with industrial GOH rail system
- Replace wire shelving with longspan shelving
- Upgrade inventory tool to Linnworks/Uphance
- Add second dedicated packing station
- Structured training of team on formal SOPs

**Total upgrade spend to Proposal 2: ~$10,000–$15,000 incremental**

## 9. Scaling Considerations

### 9.1 Scale to 1.5× (This is the maximum for Makeshift)

**At 1.5× (~4,350 pcs/month inbound, ~7,500 stock):**
- Add 10 more garment racks (brings total to 30 racks, ~2,100 hanging capacity)
- Add 4 more wire shelf units for folded stock
- Hire third warehouse associate
- Upgrade to Zoho Inventory Standard plan ($99/month) for higher order volume
- Add 1 more barcode scanner

**This is the ceiling.** Beyond 1.5× with freestanding garment racks, the operation becomes chaotic:
- Racks cannot be efficiently barcoded/bayed
- Pick travel distances become too long with no WMS guidance
- Inventory accuracy deteriorates rapidly

**At 1.5×, trigger mandatory upgrade to Proposal 2.**

### 9.2 What Happens If You Don't Upgrade

Based on industry research (Bleckmann, NetSuite, Prediko): brands that outgrow their warehouse system without upgrading typically experience:
- 5–10% inventory shrinkage (missing stock, mis-picks)
- 15–25% increase in customer returns (wrong items dispatched)
- 2–3 weeks to identify the root cause (no WMS audit trail)
- Difficulty onboarding marketplaces (Amazon, Myntra require 99%+ accuracy guarantees)
- Founder/manager spending >50% of time on warehouse firefighting instead of brand building

The makeshift model buys you 12–18 months. Treat it as exactly that — a time-boxed bridge.

## 10. Pros, Cons & Best-Fit Assessment

### Pros
- Cheapest setup of any proposal — operational in 5–7 days
- Minimal learning curve for staff — no WMS, just SOPs and a simple inventory app
- Cash preserved for product, marketing, and team
- Low commitment — if the brand pivots or consolidates, the racks can be sold/moved

### Cons
- Hard ceiling at 1.5× current volume
- Higher labour intensity (every task is manual — no automation)
- Higher error risk (dependent entirely on people following SOPs)
- No analytics: no demand forecasting, no slotting intelligence, no multi-channel sync (without Linnworks)
- Freestanding racks are space-inefficient vs. GOH rail
- Difficult to onboard wholesale buyers or marketplaces that require EDI or WMS-backed inventory data

### Best Fit
This proposal is right **only** if cash is genuinely constrained right now and the brand is within 6–12 months of confirmed revenue or funding. It is a **bridge, not a permanent solution**. If the brand is already generating steady revenue or has confirmed investment — skip directly to Proposal 2.
